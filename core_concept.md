Kerf Automation: Brand Strategy & Website Copy

1. Brand Positioning & Identity

The Name: "Kerf"
In machining, the kerf is the exact width of material removed by a blade—the precise gap required to ensure two distinct pieces interlock perfectly. Kerf Automation applies this to digital operations: calculating and removing operational waste so disparate business systems snap together seamlessly.

The Core Pitch: We bring Silicon Valley operational precision to SMB workflows. We are not just an "AI wrapper" company. We do root-cause process improvement to eliminate manual, repetitive work. We happen to use AI and code to achieve it.

Target Audience:
SMB Owners and Operations Directors. They are currently relying on inefficient methods (spreadsheets, manual tracking, disconnected software) and know there has to be a better way, but lack the technical expertise to build it.

Brand Archetype & Vibe:

Persona: Enthusiastic, detail-oriented, and highly competent.

Aesthetic: Light, airy, and precise (Google-esque). Lots of whitespace to let the content breathe.

Colors: Human-centric and approachable. Warm oranges, teals, and trustworthy blues against off-white/light gray backgrounds.

Typography: Modern, clean, geometric sans-serif (e.g., Inter, Geist, or Roboto).

3. Instructions for AI Developer

To ensure the generated site matches the Kerf Automation brand identity, adhere strictly to the following technical and design constraints:

1. Tech Stack:

Use React (Next.js preferred) and Tailwind CSS.

Use lucide-react for all icons.

If animations are needed, use Framer Motion, but keep them minimal and professional.

2. Asset Handling & Imagery:

Crucial: Do NOT use external image URLs (e.g., Unsplash) or assume local image assets exist.

Build the abstract hero graphic and any structural visuals using pure CSS (gradients, blurs, shapes) or highly stylized layouts of lucide-react icons.

3. Layout Structure:

Header: Include a sticky navbar with the Kerf logo/name on the left and a "Book Audit" CTA button on the right.

Footer: Simple footer with copyright, Kerf name, and minimal links (e.g., LinkedIn).

4. Interaction and Animation Rules:

Keep animations subtle and professional. The brand represents precision.

Use simple fade-ins on scroll and slight hover states on buttons.

Do NOT use heavy parallax, bouncy spring animations, or chaotic transitions.

5. Responsive Behavior:

Ensure the layout is strictly mobile-responsive.

Use CSS Grid/Flexbox to ensure the Hero and Flagship Solutions sections stack cleanly on screens under 768px.
